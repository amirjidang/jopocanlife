American actor Chris Evans gained wider recognition after his portrayal of Captain America in numerous MCU films. The 41 year old actor initially starred in different TV series before making his way to the big screen. He has already established himself as one of the world's highest-paid actors thanks to his films earning millions and even billions on the blockbuster worldwide. He has now accumulated a net worth of $80 million and majority of his fortune was made through his MCU films. Take a look at the highest grossing films of Chris Evans, ranked.

Fantastic Four

The film Fantastic Four was released July 8, 2005 and was distributed by 20th Century Studios, and FilmFlex. The film earned $154 million on domestic gross and made $333 million on the worldwide box office. The movie directed by Tim Story is about the scientist Reed Richards played by Ioan Gruffudd who persuaded his conceited former classmate Victor von Doom played by Julian McMahon to fund his experiments involving the cosmic energy. The crew of the experiment includes Sue Storm played by Jessica Alba, pilot Johnny Storm played by Chris Evans and astronaut Ben Grimm played by Michael Chiklis.

Captain America: The First Avenger

Captain America: The First Avenger is a film released on July 22, 2011 and was distributed by Paramount Pictures Studios, United International Pictures, and FilmFlex. The film grossed $176.6 million on domestic box office and made $370.5 million worldwide. The movie directed by Joe Johnston was set on 1941 when the world is still in throes of war. Steve Rogers played by Chris Evans wanted to take part into America's armed forces and do his part as a citizen. However he has been rejected multiple times because of his small stature. He was able to finally join after he got into the experimental program.

Thor: The Dark World

Thor: The Dark World was released on November 8, 2013 and was distributed by Walt Disney Pictures, and Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The film has made $206 million domestically and grossed $644 million on the world wide box office. The movie directed by Alan Taylor is about the gods of Asgard who are fighting against the evil race known as the Dark Elves. However a hundred years later, Jane Foster played by Israeli-American actress Natalie Portman became the host of the ultimate weapon of the gods and Thor played by Chris Hemsworth was forced to bring Foster to Asgard.

Captain America: The Winter Soldier

Captain America: The Winter Soldier is a film released on April 4, 2014 and was distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The film has made $259.7 million domestically and has grossed $714.4 million worldwide. The movie directed by the Russo brothers is about the cataclysmic events which happened in New York and together with his fellow Avengers, Captain America tries to adjust to the world of the modern times.

Spider-Man: Homecoming

Spider-Man: Homecoming is a film released on July 7, 2017 and was distributed by Sony Pictures, and Sony Pictures Releasing. The film has made $334.2 million domestically and grossed $878.3 million on the worldwide box office. The movie directed by Jon Watts is about the young Peter Parker played by British actor Tom Holland who returns home to live with Aunt May under the watchful eye of his mentor, Tony Stark. Parker finally starts to embrace his newfound identity as Spiderman.

Captain America: Civil War

Captain America: Civil War is a film released on May 6, 2016 and was distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The film has made $408 million domestically and grossed $1.15 billion on the worldwide box office. The movie directed by the Russo brothers is about applying some political pressure to the accountability system on the actions of the Avengers. This has lead to the disagreements between Iron Man who wants to adhere to the system and Captain America who believes that superheroes should have freedom to defend humanity however they see fit.

Avengers: Age of Ultron

Avengers: Age of Ultron is a film released on May 1, 2015 and was distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The film has grossed $459 million domestically and has made $1.4 billion worldwide. The movie directed by American director Joss Whedon is about Tony Stark jumpstarting a dormant peacekeeping program which went terribly wrong which forced him and the rest of the Avengers to reassemble and solve the problem.

The Avengers

The Avengers is a film released on May 4, 2012 and was distributed by Walt Disney Pictures, Paramount Pictures Studios, and Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The film grossed $623.3 million domestically and $1.515 billion worldwide. The movie directed by Joss Whedon is about the Loki gaining access to the unlimited source of power of the energy cube called the Tesseract. Nick Fury then decided to assemble a superhero team called The Avengers.

Avengers: Infinity War

Avengers: Infinity War is a film released on April 27, 2018 and was distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The film grossed $678.8 million domestically and made $2.048 billion worldwide. The movie directed by the Russo brothers is about the union of the Avengers once again to fight their battle against the most powerful enemy yet, Thanos. Thanos is on a mission to collect all the six Infinity stones to gain the ultimate power and the Avengers have to stop him from doing so.

Avengers: Endgame

Avengers: Endgame is a film released on April 26, 2019 and was distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The film grossed $858 million domestically and made $2.7 billion worldwide. The movie directed by the Russo brothers is about Avengers trying everything they can to go back in time and stop Thanos from obtaining the six stones.
