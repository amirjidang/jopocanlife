What Happened To The Cast Of Netflix's Snowflake Mountain After The Show?

It is not clear why viewers love survival reality TV shows, but the adrenaline rush from what the next scene holds may be a salient reason. While lovers of survival reality shows may be looking for more reasons why Alone is better than Survivor, a new adrenaline-pumping show is on the block. In August 2021, Netflix announced a new reality TV show at the Edinburgh TV Festival.

Snowflake Mountain features a group of privileged "kidults" in the wilderness for a survival retreat in the hopes of pushing them to become independent adults. The frustrated parents of these spoiled young cast members tricked them into going for the show. In the show, the young adults embarked on a learning curve that launched them into becoming refined adults. The survival reality show debuted in June and has been receiving positive reviews from viewers.

10 Darriea Clark Published A Book

Darriea Clark has been neck-deep in work since the show wrapped up. The view from the top of the mountain gave Darriea a new outlook on life. With this newfound insight, Darriea overcame her fear of failing and published her first book, Good Girl. Aside from becoming an author, Darriea opened up an art studio to focus on designs. These days, the TV star spends time traveling, writing, and selling secondhand clothes.

9 Randy Wentworth Is Pursuing His Passion For Wrestling.

Randy Wentworth had one of the most impressive growths on the show. The 23-year-old went from being dejected to becoming an active member cast member. Before the show, Wentworth’s relationship with his parents suffered because he dropped out of school. Things appear to be looking good for the reality star as he has finally gained the support of his parents in his career choice. Since appearing on the show, Wentworth has been traveling around the Southeast participating in wrestling matches.

8 Solomon Patterson Is An Ordained Minister

Solomon Patterson made many memories and learned a few lessons on the show. One of his valuable takeaways from Snowflake Mountain was learning to be grateful for the little things. Following his exit from the show, Solomon made many changes, including relocating from Virginia to Los Angeles and rekindling his spiritual life. Now, Solomon is an ordained minister, creating gospel-based content and offering virtual Christian Life Coaching sessions.

7 Olivia Lagaly Moved To Los Angeles

Olivia Lagaly was the first cast member to quit the show. Although Lagaly’s time on the show was short, the Ohio native learned to set boundaries and stand her ground without feeling guilty. Following her exit from the show, the reality TV star moved to Los Angeles, where she spends time on the beach while she works as a digital art freelancer and producer of the Saturday Night Live podcast.

6 Devon Smith Is Back In New York

Devon Smith is back in her city and open to trying new challenges. The party-loving city girl is looking forward to pursuing an acting career after her time on Snowflake Mountain. After filming ended, the reality TV star passed through a moment of grief from losing her best friend. The teaching she learned from the sure made her deal with her loss differently. These days, Devon is on TikTok sharing snippets of her adventurous life.

5 Deandra Joseph Is Focused On Her Business

Deandra Joseph went from being a "kidult" in the first episode of the show to bagging the $50,000 cash prize. Before joining the show, the 24-year-old was a lunch lady for middle-schoolers. The makeup artist used the money she earned from her job to support her makeup merch. Post-Snowflake Mountain, Deandra has been making swift moves in expanding her business. The reality TV star has moved to her apartment and partnered with her best friend to open a hair and makeup studio.

4 Rae Hume Wants to be On TV

Rae Hume won the heart of viewers due to her positivity and electric personality on the show. Appearing on Snowflake Mountain re-enforced Rae's innate love for being on TV. “This is the direction I want to go in! I want to do more TV work. Because I loved it,” she told RadioTimes. Rae’s awakening was not limited to her career, the reality TV star appears to have found genuine love in her boyfriend.

3 Carl Lariviere Is Healing

Carl Lariviere went from having no goals at the start show to becoming a motivated team member despite suffering from a knee injury throughout filming. On the final climbing challenge of the competition, Carl dislocated his shoulder making it the end of the road for him in the challenge. The former basketball player has made progress in his healing journey since the show closed its curtains. Asides from being on the mend, the Verge model is back into modeling.

2 Sunny Malik Is Working On A Documentary

Many viewers did not think Sunny Malik would make it past the first episode, but not only was he a finalist, but he was also the first runner-up of the competition. Sunny progressed from being a “naturally lazy person” amongst his high-achieving family to investing in his personal growth. The 26-year-old TV star has been working on getting funding for a documentary on victims of the alternative music scene. The Pennsylvania native is passionate about human rights and hopes to provoke conversations around it.

1 Liam Brown Landed His Dream Job

Liam’s biggest takeaway from Snowflake Mountain was making his own decisions without depending on input from the family. The Snowflake alum has experienced round growth since the show wrapped up. The reality TV star landed a job with the global fashion retailing brand, In the Style. Liam is maturing and has fully taken on adulthood by moving out of his family home. Liam keeps close contact with his co-stars and occasionally meets up with his British co-star, Rae.
