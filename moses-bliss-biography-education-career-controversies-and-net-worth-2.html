Moses Bliss Biography, Education, Career, Controversies, And Net Worth

Moses Bliss Uyoh Enang, popularly known as Moses Bliss, was born on February 20, 1995, in Abuja, Nigeria. At the age of 28, he has established himself as a renowned Nigerian gospel singer, worship leader, and songwriter. Moses Bliss is not only a vocal talent but also a multi-instrumentalist, proficient in playing the piano, drums, and guitar.

Moses Bliss Profile

Educational Background

Details about Moses Bliss’s formal education are not extensively documented. His focus on music and early involvement in the choir at Believers’ Loveworld church indicate an early dedication to his musical pursuits.

Career

Moses Bliss exhibited a passion for music from a tender age, commencing his formal music journey in 2017 with the release of his debut single, “E No Dey Fall My Hand.” He gained significant recognition with the release of his hit song “Too Faithful” in June 2019. Subsequently, he continued to make an impact with songs like “Bigger Everyday.” Moses Bliss’s discography includes two albums, “Too Faithful” released in May 2021 and “More Than Music (Transcendent Worship)” in February 2023.

In the realm of music distribution, he secured a deal with “Symphonic Distribution” in October 2021, further solidifying his presence in the industry. Additionally, Moses Bliss expanded his influence by founding the record label “Spotlite Nation” in February 2023, signing talented artists under its banner.

Controversies

Bliss appears to maintain a positive public image without being involved in any divisive or scandalous situations.
