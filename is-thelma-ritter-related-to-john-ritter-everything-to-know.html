Is Thelma Ritter Related To John Ritter? Everything To Know

Is Thelma Ritter by any chance related to John Ritter, an American actor? Sharing the same last name has raised inquiries about their relationship status.

Thelma was a professional American actress well known for her comedic roles and strong New York accent.

She received a Tony Award and was nominated six times for an Academy Award in the category of the best-supporting actress.

Thelma played in numerous movies like Miracle on 34th Street, A Letter to Three Wives, Titanic, and television shows like The Best of Broadway, The 20th Century-Fox Hour, and many more.

Fans became curious when they found out about the same surname Thelma and John, both the actors shared the exact surname.

Is Thelma Ritter Related To John Ritter?

No, Thelma Ritter is not related to John Ritter. Having the same surname has made many fans curious if they are related to each other by any possibility.

Thelma and John are not associated with each other. Both were in the same industry, acting. Both were famous actors of their time.

Full name Jonathan Southworth Ritter was born to his father Tex Ritter and mother Dorothy Fay. He was best known for the role of Jack Tripper on Three’s Company.

For his role as Jack Tripper, Jonathan received a Primetime Emmy Award and a Golden Globe Award.

Thelma Ritter Family And Children

Thelma Ritter family was based in Brooklyn, New York. Ritter was born to Charles and Lucy Ritter.

Actress, Ritter was married to Joseph Moran in 1972 who was also an actor who later changed his profession in the 1930s.

Ritter and Moran have two beautiful children, Monica and Joe. Ritter took a break from her acting career to raise her children.

Thelma Ritter Cause Of Death

Actress, Thelma Ritter died on 5 February 1969 due to a heart attack. At the time of her death, Thelma was in New York City.

Thelma got her final Oscar nomination for portraying the role of Elizabeth Stroud in John Frankenheimer’s movie Birdman of Alcatraz.

Ritter was nominated for an Oscar three times. The actress’ last work was on The Jerry Lewis Show on 23 January 1968.
