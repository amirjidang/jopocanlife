Family and Relatives

Marcella’s ancestors are Mexican and Cherokee. Marcella spent most of her childhood in Washington, born to a Texan father and a Coloradan mother. Marcella met her future husband, Abraham Quintanilla Jr.

He was stationed there during his military duty in the United States in October 1961 and served at Joint Base Lewis-McChord, near Tacoma. On June 8, 1963, the pair exchanged wedding vows. The family relocated to Texas after the birth of their first child on December 13, Abraham “A.B.” Issac Quintanilla III.

Abraham received his discharge papers from active duty on the same day. Marcella, her husband, and their newborn boy moved to Abraham’s hometown in Corpus Christi, Texas, within a month. They had two more daughters; the first one was Suzette Michelle, born on June 29, 1967, and the youngest one was Selena, who was welcomed to this world on April 16, 1971, at Freeport Community Hospital.

Selena is a late International Pop Singer murdered by her fan, Yolanda Saldivar, who was sentenced to life imprisonment. Abraham, who was a singer-songwriter himself, offered music schooling to all of his children. Around his three children, he established the Selena y Los Dinos band. Selena was the standout among them.

The Murder of her Daughter, Selena Quintanilla

Marcella has suffered a massive loss by losing her youngest daughter, Selena, to a gruesome murder. Selena was murdered by her fan, Yolanda Saldivar, who came into their lives when Selena’s fan club was founded in 1991 by her, a self-proclaimed admirer of Selena. In January 1994, she was promoted to manager of Selena’s shop. After some time had passed, the family learned of the money embezzlement and decided to confront her.

She created a trap for Selena and summoned her to a hotel, where she shot her in the back. She passed away on March 31, 1995. Marcella Samora and her family were heartbroken and determined to seek justice. In October 1995, Yolanda was sentenced to life in prison with the chance of parole after 30 years.
