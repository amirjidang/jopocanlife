Professional Career Of Justin Verlander

Verlander pitched a no-hitter on June 12 against the Milwaukee Brewers, flinging a fastball that reached 102 mph while striking out 12 and walking four.

On July 24, 2009, Verlander accomplished a feat that made him the first Major League starting pitcher in 24 years to face a ninth-inning or later bases-loaded situation with no one out and escape it without giving up a run.

Verlander is a member of the MLB’s Houston Astros. In MLB, Verlander has also represented the Detroit Tigers. 

Verlander, a Manakin-Sabot, Virginia native, played collegiate baseball for the Monarchs while attending Old Dominion University.

When the Detroit Tigers selected Verlander in the second round of the 2004 MLB Draft, his professional baseball career officially got underway. 

The agreement was executed on October 25, 2004. In 2005, Verlander made his career-opening appearance.

He was drafted by the Tigers in 2006 after spring training. In his first Significant League season, Verlander had a record of 17-9, a 3.63 ERA, and 124 strikeouts in 186 innings pitched.

In 2007, Verlander continued to succeed, compiling 18 victories, a 3.66 ERA, and 183 strikeouts in 2012/13 innings.

In a 6-0 victory over the Pittsburgh Pirates in 2012, Verlander carried a no-hitter into the ninth inning before surrendering a one-out single.
