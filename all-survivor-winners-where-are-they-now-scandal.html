These Survivor Winners Appear To Be Really Struggling After Being On The Show

During each season of Survivor, a group of strangers all come together to compete for the ultimate prize. Once the season ends, a sole survivor is crowned after they fought tooth and nail to get to the end and be chosen by their fellow competitors.For anyone to win a season of Survivor, they need to have a lot of social and strategic skills. With that in mind, it makes perfect sense that a lot of Survivor winners have gone on to great success.Related: 7 ‘Survivor’ Moments That Went Beyond The Game And Became RealNo matter how many positive attributes a person may have, everyone is capable of making mistakes. Unfortunately for some Survivor winners, they made major mistakes after they prevailed on the show.

6 What Happened To Judson "Fabio" Birza After Winning Survivor

Among Survivor's biggest fans, the show's 21st season which took place in Nicaragua is often cited as one of its worst. While there are many reasons for that, including bad twists and quitting players, a lot of viewers were unimpressed with the season's winner.

Not long after Judson "Fabio" Birza became the sole survivor, things went awry for him. Arrested in Santa Monica for skateboarding while under the influence, it also turned out that Fabio was on probation at the time and charged with violating it.

Related: 10 Reality Stars Who've Done Time In Jail (& Why)

Released soon after his arrest, Judson "Fabio" Birza was picked up from jail in a limo. Since that incident, Fabio has managed to stay out of the headlines and drama.

5 What Happened To Aras Baskauskas After Winning Survivor

During the 12th season of Survivor which took place in Panama, Aras Baskauaskas prevailed over his chief rival Terry Deitz to win. After that, Baskauaskas returned for the Blood vs. Water season several years later alongside his brother Vytas.

Even though Aras Baskauaskas was returned to Survivor for a second season, he told the Irvine News how much he struggled after was thrust into fame.

"We all have our personal barriers. After I won Survivor, I got really depressed. It was just too much. Too much exposure to the elements, to myself, to the world. I couldn't vibrate that high for that long."

Fortunately for Baskauaskas, he went on to be more at peace with himself after founding a yoga studio, getting marryied, and becoming a father of two.

4 What Happened To Jenna Morasca After Winning Survivor

During the sixth season of Survivor which took place in the Amazon, fans were introduced to one of the show's all-time greats, Rob Cesternino. However, the season's winner was Jenna Morasca.

Later brought back for the first All-Star season, Morasca left the show early fearing that her mother would soon pass away. Since then some Survivor fans have come to believe that Jeff Probst's reaction to Morasca's All-Stars departure could be his worst moment ever.

Unfortunately for Jenna Morasca, one of her worst moments came many years later in 2018.

After Washington, PA police found Jenna Morasca passed out in a running vehicle, they arrested her for DUI and possession of a controlled substance. On top of that, Morasca reportedly was given Narcan and tried to fight and bite the police.

3 What Happened To Brian Heidik After Winning Survivor

When looking back at Survivor history, several of the show's competitors have been labeled villains. Even with that in mind, there still are people who would argue that the winner of the show's fifth season from Thailand is the most ruthless Survivor ever.

Related: The 10 Best Villains In Survivor History, Ranked

After Brian Heidik won the fifth season of Survivor, he later found himself embroiled in controversy. At the time of his Survivor win, Heidik was in a marriage that later made headlines due to accusations of domestic violence.

In 2002, Brian Heidik's then-wife Charmaine Costigan was arrested for allegedly smacking him in the nose. Four years later, Heidik was the one arrested on misdemeanor battery and animal cruelty charges.

Unsurprisingly, Brian Heidik and Charmaine Costigan's marriage wound up ending in divorce. Since then, the Survivor winner got married to another woman named Courtney Heidik.

2 What Happened To Richard Hatch After Winning Survivor

The very first Survivor villain, during the show's first season Richard Hatch became infamous on his way to being named the sole survivor. Little did anyone know, Hatch would go on to pay a huge price for his Survivor win.

When Richard Hatch returned for the first All-Stars season, he was at the center of a disturbing controversy. After a challenge, Hatch's fellow competitor Susan Hawk stated that he thrust his unclothed body at her in a way that made her feel "dehumanized".

After Richard Hatch's final Survivor appearance, the controversy kept coming. According to the IRS, Hatch didn't pay taxes on the prize money he received for winning Survivor. For his part, Hatch has always maintained he did nothing wrong since CBS agreed to pay the taxes.

No matter what the truth of who was at fault for the unpaid taxes, Richard Hatch is the one who paid the price. Convicted of tax evasion in 2006, Hatch served 51 months in prison. Later, Hatch got in trouble for a probation violation and spent another 9 months behind bars.

1 What Happened To Todd Herzog After Winning Survivor

During the 15th season of Survivor which took place in China, Todd Herzog played a truly fantastic game. Especially impressive during the final tribal council, Herzog proved how smart and personable he could be.

Tragically, after Survivor: China aired, Todd Herzog became an alcoholic. After revealing how bad his struggles had become, Todd Herzog made an appearance on Dr. Phil in which he was so inebriated that he needed help to walk onstage.

To say that there was a lot of concern for Todd Herzog's well-being after his Dr. Phil appearance is a massive understatement. Herzog went on to enter rehab more than once and suffer relapses just like most people who have battled addiction.

In 2017, Todd Herzog went on "Rob Has a Podcast" and revealed he was sober and from all accounts that has remained the case ever since. In 2019, Herzog got engaged to Jess Hermansen and the following year the couple tied the knot.
