Terry Bradshaw Health: His Battle With Cancer &amp; Recovery

There was a noticeable difference in Hall of Fame quarterback Terry Bradshaw last month when the Fox Sports NFL Sunday team returned for the start of the season. 

For starters, he appeared heavier, and during last week’s program, he was made aware of the need to address his health issues after an incident. 

Terry Bradshaw, a member of the Pro Football Hall of Fame and a FOX Sports analyst, disclosed on Sunday that he has spent much of the past year fighting cancer, but that he is now cancer-free, noting recent inquiries about his work on the studio program.

Speaking on Fox NFL Sunday, Bradshaw stated, ‘Last week on the show, I ran out of breath and (co-host) Howie (Long) lifted me up. Many people have questioned what’s wrong with me and what has physically occurred to me. I was diagnosed withh bladder cancer in November. I sought surgery and medical care at Yale University Medical Center. I have no bladder cancer as of right now. That’s excellent news, I guess. Then in March, I had a bad neck, so I had an MRI. We then discovered a Merkel cell tumour, a rare type of skin cancer, in my left neck. At the MD Anderson Cancer Center in Houston, Bradshaw claimed to have undergone surgery to remove it.’

According to the Mayo Clinic, Merkel cell carcinoma is a rare kind of skin cancer that typically manifests as a flesh-colored or bluish-red nodule, frequently on your face, head, or neck. 

Neuroendocrine carcinoma of the skin is also called Merkel cell cancer. 

This particular type of cancer most frequently arises in older persons and tends to grow swiftly and spread quickly to other sections of your body. 

Whether or not Merkel cell carcinoma has metastasized outside of the skin frequently affects the treatment choices available.
