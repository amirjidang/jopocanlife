Les McCann: The Early Years and Musical Roots

Les McCann, a prominent figure in the world of jazz, left an indelible mark with his innovative contributions to the genre. To truly understand the man behind the music, one must delve into his roots.

Birth and Early Years in Lexington, Kentucky

Les McCann was born on September 23, 1935, in the picturesque city of Lexington, Kentucky. From the very beginning, McCann was immersed in a musical environment.

Raised in a family that appreciated the artistry of jazz, his father’s love for the genre and his siblings’ involvement in church choirs laid the foundation for his musical journey.

A Prodigy at the Piano

Les McCann’s musical journey began at an astonishingly young age.

By the age of 3, he had already developed an affinity for the piano, showcasing an innate talent that hinted at the extraordinary career that lay ahead.

Military Service and California Influences

Les McCann’s life took a pivotal turn when he joined the U.S. Navy. Stationed in California, he found himself in the midst of a vibrant and diverse music scene that would shape his artistic sensibilities.

During this time, McCann was particularly influenced by the music of pianist Erroll Garner and trumpeter Miles Davis, two giants in the jazz world.

The Impact of Erroll Garner and Miles Davis

Erroll Garner’s virtuoso piano skills and Miles Davis’s innovative approach to the trumpet left an indelible mark on Les McCann.

Their influence played a crucial role in shaping his evolving musical style, contributing to the fusion of jazz with other genres that would become his signature sound.
