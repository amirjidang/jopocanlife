Early Life

Kerry King, best known as the co-founder, co-lead guitarist, and songwriter of the thrash metal band Slayer, was born on June 3, 1964, in Los Angeles, California. He developed a passion for playing the guitar at a young age, starting his musical journey at the age of 13. As a teenager, King attended three different high schools, which shaped his early experiences and exposed him to diverse influences.

Slayer

Kerry King co-founded the legendary thrash metal band Slayer, widely recognized as one of the most influential and iconic bands in the genre. As the co-lead guitarist and songwriter, Kerry King played a vital role in shaping Slayer’s distinctive sound and dark lyrical themes throughout their career.

With the band, Kerry King recorded numerous albums that solidified Slayer’s place in metal history. One of their most notable albums is “Reign of Blood,” which is often regarded as one of the greatest metal albums ever made. Known for its aggressive riffs, intense drumming, and controversial lyrics, the album epitomizes Slayer’s signature style.

“Slayer’s music hits you like a punch in the face. It’s raw, brutal, and unapologetic.” – Metal Hammer

Slayer’s music pushed the boundaries of thrash metal, blending elements of speed, aggression, and dark imagery. Their relentless energy and powerful performances earned them a dedicated following and a legendary status in the metal community.

However, after an illustrious career spanning four decades, Slayer announced their disbandment in 2019, leaving behind a legacy that continues to inspire new generations of metalheads.

Other Appearances

Kerry King’s talent has not been limited to his work with Slayer. Throughout his career, he has made notable guest appearances and collaborations with various artists, showcasing his versatility and musical prowess.

In addition to the Beastie Boys, Kerry King also collaborated with the Swedish extreme metal band Witchery. His guitar work on their album “Don’t Fear the Reaper” brought a heightened level of intensity and precision to the music.

Furthermore, in the mid-80s, Kerry King joined forces with thrash metal band Megadeth for several live performances. This collaboration allowed fans to witness the combined power of two legendary guitarists, Kerry King and Megadeth’s Dave Mustaine.

“Collaborations with other artists allowed me to explore different musical territories and push the boundaries of my playing. It’s always exciting to work with talented musicians from different genres and create something unique,” says Kerry King.

These guest appearances and collaborations further solidified Kerry King’s status as an influential figure in the metal scene. His willingness to experiment and venture beyond the confines of his main band showcased his versatility and artistic growth.
