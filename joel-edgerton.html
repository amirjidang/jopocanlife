Biography Timeline

Joel Edgerton is an Australian actor and filmmaker known for his powerful performances in independent and mainstream films. He was born on June 23, 1974, in Blacktown, New South Wales, Australia.

  • 1995: Edgerton graduated from The Hills Grammar School in Sydney and attended Nepean Drama School in Western Sydney.
  • 1996: He made his professional debut in the TV series “Police Rescue” and appeared in several other Australian TV shows.
  • 1999: Edgerton starred in his first film, “Praise,” which received critical acclaim at Cannes.
  • 2002: He gained international recognition for his role in the film “The Secret Life of Us,” which aired on Australian television and was later shown in the United States.
  • 2005: Edgerton made his Hollywood debut in “Star Wars: Episode III – Revenge of the Sith” as the character Owen Lars.
  • 2008: He co-wrote and starred in the Australian film “The Square,” which was nominated for several awards at the Sundance Film Festival.
  • 2010: Edgerton starred in the film “Animal Kingdom,” which received critical acclaim and numerous award nominations.
  • 2011: He starred in the film “Warrior” and received critical praise for his performance as a mixed martial artist.
  • 2013: Edgerton wrote, directed, and starred in the film “Felony.”
  • 2015: He starred in the film “Black Mass” alongside Johnny Depp and in the movie “The Gift,” which he also wrote, directed, and produced.
  • 2017: Edgerton starred in the critically acclaimed film “Loving,” for which he received a Golden Globe nomination.
  • 2018: He starred in the Netflix film “Bright” and the film “Red Sparrow” alongside Jennifer Lawrence.
  • 2019: Edgerton starred in the film “The King” and the TV series “The Underground Railroad.”
  • 2021: He starred in the TV series “Nine Perfect Strangers.”

Edgerton has become known for his versatile acting skills and ability to take on various roles. In addition to his successful acting career, he has made a name for himself as a writer and director.
