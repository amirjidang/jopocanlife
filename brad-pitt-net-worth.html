Brad Pitt's Production Company

While Brad Pitt's acting career is undeniably impressive, his influence extends far beyond the silver screen.

In 2001, he co-founded Plan B Entertainment with his ex-wife, actress Jennifer Aniston. Pitt took sole ownership following the couple's divorce in 2005.

Plan B boasts a remarkable catalog, including Academy Award darlings like Moonlight, 12 Years A Slave, and The Departed. These films tackle diverse themes and social issues, highlighting Pitt's commitment to meaningful storytelling beyond mainstream blockbusters.

He's also championed projects like Selma, The Underground Railroad, and If Beale Street Could Talk, amplifying voices and experiences often marginalized in Hollywood.

Plan B movies have generated $3 billion in global box office revenue against $1 billion in total production budgets.

According to a report by a Forbes blogger, Pitt earned over $113 million by selling a majority stake in his Plan B production company in 2022 to French media conglomerate Mediawan.

The deal reportedly valued Plan B at $300 million.

Brad Pitt's Wine Ventures

Brad Pitt's empire extends far beyond the big screen, encompassing both luxurious tastes and the fashion industry.

He's poured his passion into two distinct enterprises: Château Miraval, a renowned rosé winery in France, and God's True Cashmere, a high-end designer clothing line.

In 2008, Pitt and his former partner bought a controlling interest in Miraval, in the village of Correns in south-east France, between Marseille and Nice.

The Oscar-winning former couple purchased the 1,300-acre property for around $28 million. The main house on the property reportedly has 35 rooms, a moat, vineyards, trees, several guest houses, a recording studio and a pine forest surrounding it.

The wine business achieved swift success, with profits soaring from $1.3 million in 2013 to over $19 million in 2022. In 2022, Brad sued his ex-wife Angelina Jolie for selling her share of the Château Miraval to a Russian oligarch, which Pitt maintained violated his contractual rights.

Pitt also expanded his entrepreneurial portfolio by launching his designer brand, God's True Cashmere. Collaborating with jewelry designer Sat Hari, the brand debuted at London's Selfridges, offering luxurious pieces with meticulous attention to detail.

With prices starting at $1,640 for gemstone-buttoned long-sleeved shirts adorned with gemstone buttons, each design is meticulously crafted with symbolic elements like seven snaps representing the seven chakras.

Brad Pitt's Philanthropic Efforts

The Fight Club actor is famous for his philanthropic efforts. His commitment to social justice and positive change shines through in the many causes he champions.

In 2006, Pitt and Jolie flew to Haiti, where they visited a school supported by Yéle Haïti, a charity founded by Haitian-born hip-hop musician Wyclef Jean. In May 2007, Pitt and Jolie donated $1 million to three organizations in Chad and Sudan dedicated to those affected by the crisis in the Darfur region.

One of his most notable efforts, the Make It Right Foundation, was established in 2006 with a $5 million commitment from both Pitt and philanthropist Steve Bing.

According to The Hollywood Reporter, the foundation poured $8.5 million into constructing 150 sustainable, affordable homes in New Orleans' Lower Ninth Ward after Hurricane Katrina's devastation.

Pitt and his ex-wife Angelina Jolie joined forces in 2006 to establish the Jolie-Pitt Foundation, tackling global humanitarian issues head-on with initial $1 million donations to Global Action for Children and Doctors Without Borders, followed by another $100,000 to the Daniel Pearl Foundation.

He's a vocal advocate and generous donor to Global Green USA, championing environmental sustainability with donations like his $1 million contribution to their Hurricane Sandy relief efforts.

He readily lends his star power to raise awareness and funds for mission critical organizations like Doctors Without Borders.
