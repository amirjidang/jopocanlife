University of Iowa Head Coach Salaries

The University of Iowa is renowned for its exceptional sports programs, and the salaries of its head coaches reflect their importance. These talented individuals not only lead their respective teams to victory but also play a crucial role in shaping the university’s athletic legacy. In 2022, Kirk J Ferentz, the head coach of the football team, earned an impressive annual wage of $6,898,710.00. This remarkable figure stands as the highest reported salary for a state employee in Iowa, underlining the significance of the position and the immense responsibility it entails.

In addition to Kirk J Ferentz, other head coaches at the University of Iowa also command substantial salaries. For instance, Francis John McCaffery, the head coach of the men’s basketball team, and Gunasekaran Suresh, the head coach of the men’s tennis team, also exceeded $3 million in annual earnings. These salaries not only reflect the university’s commitment to attracting top coaching talent but also recognize the immense value these head coaches bring to the university’s athletic programs.

Iowa State University Head Coach Salaries

When it comes to head coach salaries at Iowa State University, the numbers are significant. Matthew Allen Campbell, the head football coach, earned an impressive $2,950,500.00 in 2022. This places him among the highest earners among state employees in Iowa. It’s clear that coaching at Iowa State University comes with substantial financial rewards.

But Campbell is not the only head coach at Iowa State University who commands a handsome salary. Steven Marshall Prohm, the head basketball coach, received a salary above $1 million, showing the university’s commitment to investing in top coaching talent. Thomas John Otzelberger, the head coach of the men’s basketball team, also earned a salary above $1 million, further demonstrating the competitive compensation packages available at Iowa State University.
